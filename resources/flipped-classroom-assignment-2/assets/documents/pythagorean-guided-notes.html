1. Label the Right Triangle

Label the two legs as a and b. Label the hypotenuse as c.

2. Formula

a2 + b2 = c2

The legs are the two sides that make the right angle. The hypotenuse is across from the right angle.

3. Area of Squares

Squaring a side length gives the area of a square built on that side.

  • Square on leg a: area is a2.
  • Square on leg b: area is b2.
  • Square on hypotenuse c: area is c2.

4. Steps for Finding a Hypotenuse

  1. Identify the two legs and the hypotenuse.
  2. Square each leg.
  3. Add the two square areas.
  4. Find the positive side length whose square equals that total.
  5. State the hypotenuse with units when units are given.

5. Completed Example: 3-4-5

Legs: 3 and 4

32 + 42 = c2

9 + 16 = c2

25 = c2

c = 5
