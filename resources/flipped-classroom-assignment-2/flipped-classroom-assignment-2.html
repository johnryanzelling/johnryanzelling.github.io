Lesson Overview

This flipped lesson introduces the Pythagorean theorem as a bridge from familiar area ideas to right-triangle distance problems. It is designed for one 60-minute in-person class period after a short, purposeful pre-class video sequence.

Essential question: How can the areas of squares help determine an unknown distance in a right triangle?

  • Topic: Pythagorean theorem foundations
  • Audience: Regular Grade 7 Mathematics students who need remedial pre-algebra bridge support
  • Class time: One 60-minute in-person lesson
  • Estimated pre-class time: 18-22 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the legs and hypotenuse of a right triangle.
  • Find a missing hypotenuse when both legs are known.
  • Determine whether familiar side lengths form a right triangle.
  • Apply the theorem to simple real-world distance problems.

Required Materials

  • School laptop with internet access.
  • Headphones for video viewing.
  • Guided notes for recording examples and questions.
  • Pencil and scratch paper; no calculator should be necessary.

Prior Knowledge

  • Area of a square.
  • Side length of a square when the area is known.
  • Area of a right triangle as half of an enclosing rectangle or square.
  • Solving one-step equations.
Bridge lesson note: This lesson does not assume students have already mastered the Pythagorean theorem. It uses perfect squares and familiar triples to build readiness for the standard expectation.

Before Class

Prepare for the Lesson

Complete this short sequence before class so we can use class time for questions, guided practice, and the escape-room challenge instead of replaying the whole lecture.

  1. Watch the teacher mini lesson first. Focus on the vocabulary of right angle, legs, and hypotenuse.
  2. Watch the required external videos. Use them to reinforce the area model and to see why the theorem works.
  3. Use the guided notes. Record the key examples, including 3-4-5, 6-8-10, and the 5-12-13 pause problem.
  4. Complete the readiness check. This helps show what you understand before the in-class practice begins.
  5. Bring one question to class. Write down one point that felt confusing, surprising, or worth asking about.
Accountability: Students who do not finish the pre-class work will begin at a short catch-up station. The class period will not be used to replay the full lecture, and completion will be checked separately from understanding.

During Class

In-Class Learning Flow

Class time will begin with a brief warm-up and teacher check-in, then shift into supported practice before students move into the escape-room activity.

  1. 0-5 minutes: individual readiness problem.
  2. 5-12 minutes: targeted review based on pre-class needs.
  3. 12-17 minutes: group assignments and directions.
  4. 17-47 minutes: collaborative escape room.
  5. 47-54 minutes: solution review and error discussion.
  6. 54-58 minutes: individual exit ticket.
  7. 58-60 minutes: student survey.

Five-Minute Catch-Up Station

If You Missed the Pre-Class Work

Start here while the rest of the class begins the warm-up. This station catches you up without replaying the full lecture for the whole class.

  3. Review the 3-4-5 example.
  4. Answer one readiness question.

Access

Accessibility

This lesson uses semantic sections, keyboard-reachable links and controls, visible focus states, live feedback regions, accessible SVG diagrams, and reduced-motion support. Escape-room progress is optional local browser storage only.

Media and Reading Supports

  • Teacher-video captions are supported when a local captions file is configured.
  • The teacher-video transcript link appears when a transcript path is configured; otherwise the video slot gives an honest unconfigured notice.
  • Printable guided notes and written worked examples are available for students who need a paper path.
  • External video embeds include direct links for environments that block embeds.

Activity Access

  • Printable escape-room sheet and printable exit ticket support offline or paper-based work.
  • Controls are keyboard accessible with visible focus and live feedback announcements.
  • Reduced-motion CSS is included for students who request less motion.
  • Diagrams include text alternatives and visible right-angle markers.

Classroom Access

  • No calculator is required; numbers use familiar triples and whole-number arithmetic.
  • No student account is required for the custom GitHub Pages lesson.
  • The school-based catch-up station supports students who missed the pre-class work.
  • If localStorage is unavailable, the page remains usable during the visit and reports that progress may not survive refresh.

Educator Accountability

Accountability Plan

The flipped structure preserves class time for collaborative problem-solving instead of passive screen use. Completion and mastery are tracked separately so students are supported without turning the class period into a repeated lecture.

  • Students are expected to complete the pre-class video sequence, guided notes, and readiness check before class.
  • The teacher reviews readiness responses at the beginning of class and chooses a short targeted review.
  • Students who are unprepared complete the five-minute catch-up station, then join a group.
  • The whole class does not rewatch the complete lecture.
  • Completion and mastery are documented separately through readiness work, group reasoning, and the individual exit ticket.
  • Repeated statements such as "I will do it at home" require teacher follow-up and a plan for completion.
