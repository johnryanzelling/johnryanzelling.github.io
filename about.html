Teaching Philosophy

I believe technology should support instruction with purpose. A digital tool should not be used only because it is new, popular, or visually interesting. It should help students understand content more clearly, practice skills more effectively, communicate ideas, collaborate with others, or demonstrate what they know. But tools should also be used if it makes learning fun!

My teaching philosophy is grounded in clarity, structure, and accessibility. Students benefit when lessons are organized, expectations are clear, and learning tools are chosen intentionally. In mathematics and STEM-related learning, technology can be especially valuable when it makes abstract ideas more visual, supports guided practice, provides immediate feedback, or gives students more than one way to engage with content.

This Digital Toolbox reflects that philosophy. Each tool included in the toolbox should have a realistic classroom purpose, a clear instructional connection, and the potential to support student learning rather than distract from it.
